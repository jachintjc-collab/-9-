Video Script

Vivien's stage 2 defeat — chapter 3 final scene

After the chapter 3 Plant 43 hybrid boss is defeated, Vivien herself stays in the lab. The closing scene of chapter 3 — about three minutes — resolves Vivien's arc within RE9. The scene's restraint is what makes it work.

My take:Vivien's final scene is RE's most quiet character ending. No redemption arc. No villain comeuppance. Just a person facing the end of their choices.

The full transcript

[Grace returns to Vivien's lab after the boss fight. The lab is structurally damaged but the workstation is intact. Vivien is at the terminal, finishing what appears to be a final log entry.]

[Vivien hears Grace enter. Doesn't turn around.]

VIVIEN: "It's done?"

GRACE: "It's down."

VIVIEN: "Down or destroyed?"

GRACE: "Destroyed. The lab containment is holding."

[Vivien types for another moment. Finishes. Saves.]

VIVIEN: "I've encrypted my full research records. Both the cures and the failures. The decryption keys are with my legal counsel. They release publicly in seventy-two hours unless I personally rescind."

GRACE: "Why publicly?"

VIVIEN: "Because the seventeen patients I helped deserve to be a real medical legacy, and the people I failed deserve to be on the record. Both happened. Both should be remembered."

[Beat.]

VIVIEN: "Grace, what I did at Care Center... I want you to publish my methodology too. The mistakes. So no one else makes them."

GRACE: "That's not how containment works. The methodology stays classified. It would be reused."

VIVIEN: "Then teach the next researchers the failure modes. Privately. But teach them."

GRACE: "BSAA already has the failure modes from your records. I'll make sure they're used."

[Vivien stands. Walks to a sealed cabinet. Opens it. Inside: a small case containing what appears to be a final medical dose.]

VIVIEN: "I have an option, Grace. The mold doesn't take me, but I made other preparations."

GRACE: "You don't have to do this."

VIVIEN: "I know. That's exactly why I'm doing it."

[Vivien takes the dose. The reaction is fast — no slow drama. Within fifteen seconds she's lowered herself into her workstation chair and her breathing stops.]

[Grace stands in the doorway. Doesn't speak. After a moment, she lowers her weapon. Holsters it.]

GRACE (alone in the lab): "I'll publish your methodology. And your name."

[Cut: Grace exits the Care Center. The chapter ends.]

What the scene resolves

Vivien's arc closes within chapter 3. She doesn't escape. She doesn't get killed by Grace. She chooses her own ending. The research records get published. Her seventeen successful cases will be part of the public medical literature. Her failures will be too.

This is unusual for RE. Most RE villains either escape (Wesker, Saddler), get killed in boss fights, or die in unclear circumstances that allow possible return. Vivien definitively ends, and on her own terms.

The seventy-two hour public release

Vivien's encrypted research records publishing publicly in seventy-two hours is the scene's most institutionally important detail. This forces BSAA to deal with public knowledge of the Care Center research. The institutional cover-up Grace investigated becomes impossible to maintain.

This is the scene that makes RE9's BSAA institutional plot real. Without Vivien's records going public, BSAA could continue cover-up. With them going public, the institutional crisis Grace is investigating becomes BSAA's problem to manage.

Voice work

Laila Berzins (Vivien) plays the scene with the same exhausted control as the reveal scene. The "I'm doing it because I don't have to" line is the closing performance anchor — Vivien chooses agency over necessity.

Cissy Jones (Grace) plays witnessing. No intervention attempt. No emotional response. Professional respect for Vivien's choice. The closing "I'll publish your methodology. And your name." line is the scene's quiet promise.

Korean dub notes

The Korean voice work on Vivien's final scene was widely praised on Naver. Korean readers responded particularly to the ethical framing — Vivien's choice as moral agency rather than escape from consequences. The Korean rendering of "I'm doing it because I don't have to" — "할 필요가 없으니까 하는 거야" — preserved the philosophical weight.

Production notes

The scene's pace was carefully calibrated. Earlier drafts had longer death sequence and more dialogue. The final version cuts both. Vivien's death takes fifteen seconds; she's lowered to the chair and breathing stops without theatrical lingering.

This was Nakanishi's specific direction. The death needed to be quiet, not dramatic. The dramatic moment had already happened in the choice to take the dose; the death itself should be procedural.

What this sets up for later

The published methodology in BSAA hands. The Mia Winters samples question (Vivien used Mia's samples in her research; this gets resolved in chapter 7 Mia cameo). The institutional crisis at BSAA (will pay off in chapter 6 and 8). The seventeen surviving patients (potential RE10 thread).

The seventeen patients thread for RE10

Vivien's seventeen surviving patients are not mentioned again in RE9 after this scene. RE10 has the option to feature them — some may have long-term complications from the gene therapy. The setup is there for whichever direction RE10 wants.

Korean fan theory threads on Naver have speculated about the patient seventeen returning as RE10 secondary character. The setup supports this possibility without committing to it.

My take

Vivien's stage 2 defeat is the chapter 3 closer that earns the entire Vivien arc. If you played RE9 and skipped this cutscene, you don't have the Vivien story Capcom actually wrote. The scene rewards careful watching. It also models a kind of villain ending RE has never done before.
