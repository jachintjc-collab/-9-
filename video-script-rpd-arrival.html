Video Script

RPD arrival scene (chapter 6) — Grace returns to Raccoon City

Grace's first scene at the rebuilt RPD facility is RE9's most-loaded callback to series history. The scene runs about six minutes and packs more series-fan rewards per minute than any other cutscene in the game.

My take:The RPD arrival is RE9's emotional centerpiece. If you played RE2 Remake, this scene hits harder than anything else in the game. Capcom earned the callback through restraint earlier in RE9.

The full transcript

[Wide shot: Grace's car approaches the new RPD facility. The building was reconstructed in 2010 on the original RPD foundation, in the new Raccoon City built around the ARK facility.]

[Grace parks. Steps out. Stands at the entrance.]

[She looks up. Camera pans up the facade. The statue (recreation of the original) is in place.]

GRACE (voice-over): "I haven't been here since I was nine."

[Cut: interior lobby. The recreation of the original RE2 main hall, with modern security additions. The statue. The desk where Marvin Branagh died in the original 1998 outbreak.]

OFFICER (Captain Watkins): "Ms. Ashcroft?"

GRACE: "Yes."

WATKINS: "Captain Watkins, RPD New. Leon S. Kennedy called ahead. He said you'd want to see the archive personally."

GRACE: "He said I'd want to see the eastern wing."

[Pause. Watkins recognizes the test.]

WATKINS: "He did say that. I should have anticipated. This way."

[They walk through the lobby. Grace pauses at the statue.]

GRACE: "It's a recreation."

WATKINS: "The original was destroyed in the 1998 incident. The recreation was commissioned in 2010 from the original sculptor's surviving sketches."

GRACE: "Did Sherry approve?"

WATKINS: "...You know about Sherry Birkin's involvement?"

GRACE: "Leon told me. She funded most of the reconstruction."

[Beat.]

WATKINS: "Ms. Birkin specifically requested the statue be recreated. She said her father would have wanted it."

[Grace doesn't respond. They continue walking.]

[Cut: eastern wing. Old wooden door. Modern keypad lock.]

WATKINS: "This door connects to the original RPD basement archives that survived the fire. Climate-controlled now. Cleared for your access."

[Grace inputs a code. The door opens.]

GRACE (alone after Watkins leaves): "Okay. Day six."

What the scene establishes

The RPD has been rebuilt. Sherry Birkin (RE2 child character, last seen in RE6) has been involved in the reconstruction. The original Marvin Branagh death site has been preserved. Grace's family connection to Raccoon City is now confirmed (she "hasn't been here since I was nine" — 23 years prior to RE9's 2026 setting puts her at age 9 in 2003, post-Raccoon-destruction).

The Watkins exchange establishes the test Grace runs to verify operatives know the deal. The "eastern wing" reference is from a never-released Leon-Sherry private communication. Watkins passing the test means he's on Leon's vetted contact list.

What the scene rewards series fans

The Marvin Branagh callback is the deepest cut. RE2 Remake players will recognize the lobby. The statue recreation discussion rewards anyone who knows RE2's lobby statue history. Sherry Birkin's involvement is a major lore extension — she's been off-page since RE6, this confirms she's still operating in the broader RE world.

For new players: the scene works as standalone (you don't need to know any of this to follow). For series fans: the scene is dense with rewards.

Voice work in the scene

Cissy Jones (Grace) and the actor playing Captain Watkins (uncredited Capcom internal cast) establish their dynamic in two exchanges. Watkins is competent professional. Grace tests him. He passes. They both know what just happened.

The voice direction emphasizes restraint. Neither character gets emotional in dialogue. The emotional weight is in what they don't say.

Korean dub notes

This scene's Korean dub got the longest production schedule of any chapter 6 content. Korean voice direction wanted the Watkins-Grace recognition exchange to read as professional vetting, not as suspicion. The Korean rendering succeeded — Naver discussion specifically praised the scene as one of the best Korean dub moments in the game.

Sherry Birkin's name in Korean ("셰리 버킨") was a deliberate Capcom Korea decision — they confirmed earlier RE Korean localization spelling rather than re-romanizing. Korean RE veterans appreciated the continuity.

Production notes

The RPD lobby recreation in RE9 reuses the asset library from RE2 Remake with modern additions. The art team specifically wanted players who'd played RE2 Remake to recognize the space immediately. The modern security additions (the keypad locks, the climate control rooms) signal the reconstruction without breaking the visual memory.

The Sherry Birkin reference was Capcom Japan's narrative team call. Internal discussion considered whether to reveal Sherry's involvement here or save it for RE10. The decision to put it in RE9 was made specifically to set up RE10's BSAA institutional plot.

What the scene sets up for later

The basement archives become a major chapter 6 location. The original 1998 documents Grace accesses there contain her brother's case file (the reveal pays off the chapter 1 photo). Sherry Birkin's mention will be paid off in RE10 (confirmed by Nakanishi at GDC 2026).

My take

The RPD arrival is the chapter 6 highlight and the game's best fanservice scene. Watch it carefully. The Sherry Birkin reference alone changes the lore implications for the next decade of RE games.
