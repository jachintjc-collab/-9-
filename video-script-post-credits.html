RE9 post-credits scene — full transcript + RE10 setup reading

RE9's post-credits scene is brief — under one minute — but consequential. It's the franchise's most direct statement about what the next game will be. Players who closed the credits early missed important RE10 setup.

My take:The post-credits scene is mandatory viewing for anyone planning to play RE10. It also reveals which character will be RE10's primary protagonist — most theorists are guessing wrong because they didn't watch carefully.

The full transcript

[Credits complete. Brief black screen. Audio fades up: rain, distant city sounds.]

[Fade in: night exterior. Seoul, South Korea. The Han River is visible in the background. Modern skyline.]

[Cut: a small apartment interior. A woman in her early thirties — short black hair, professional dress, BSAA field operative posture — finishes a phone call.]

WOMAN (in Korean, with English subtitles): "Yes, sir. I understand. I'll be in Boston tomorrow."

[She hangs up. Walks to a desk. Opens a folder. The first page is Grace Ashcroft's publication — the public version of Grace's RE9 findings.]

[The woman turns the page. The second page is a BSAA internal memo with classified markings. Top of the memo: SUBJECT: GRACE ASHCROFT. STATUS: ACTIVE THREAT TO INSTITUTIONAL SECURITY. DISPOSITION: TBD.]

[The woman reads briefly. Then closes the folder.]

[She walks to the window. Looks out at the Seoul skyline.]

WOMAN (in Korean, quietly): "I will not let this stand."

[Camera pulls back to show her reflection in the window — face fully visible. A small badge on her lapel: BSAA Field Commander rank.]

[Fade to black. Single title card: RESIDENT EVIL 10 — IN DEVELOPMENT]

What the scene establishes

RE10's primary protagonist is a Korean-speaking BSAA field commander based in Seoul. She is not aligned with the BSAA faction that wants Grace eliminated. She represents an internal BSAA opposition or reform faction. Her mission to Boston (where Grace and Sherry Birkin are working) is to assist, not threaten.

The character is unnamed in this scene. Capcom is reserving the name reveal for marketing closer to RE10's release.

Why this confirms RE10's Korean setting (partial)

The Seoul exterior framing establishes RE10's connection to Korea. Even if RE10's primary plot location is elsewhere (Boston is implied), Korean operations will be significant. This is the franchise's most explicit Korean storyline acknowledgment.

This validates Capcom's heavy Korean localization investment for RE9. The investment was setup for RE10's Korean character introduction.

The "active threat to institutional security" framing

The BSAA internal memo designating Grace as institutional security threat confirms the true ending's "contingency" reference. The hostile BSAA faction has formalized opposition to Grace. RE10's central conflict will involve this institutional faction directly.

The new Korean protagonist is positioned as opposition to the hostile faction. She'll be Grace's ally, possibly Sherry Birkin's ally, working against BSAA internal corruption.

Voice work

The new protagonist is voiced (in Korean) by a confirmed Korean voice actress (name not yet released; Capcom Korea press release coming closer to RE10 announcement). Her English voice for international release versions has not been cast as of this writing.

The "I will not let this stand" line in Korean — "이걸 그냥 두지 않을 거예요" — was specifically composed to be the character's signature line. The phrasing has been quietly trending in Korean RE community since launch.

Korean community reception

The post-credits scene generated the largest single Korean fan reaction to RE9. Naver discussion threads on the new protagonist hit multiple thousand-comment threads within 24 hours of the game's launch. Korean RE fans have been waiting for a Korean lead character since RE2 era.

The unanimous Korean reception was positive. Capcom investing in a Korean protagonist (not just Korean translation) is the franchise milestone Korean fans have been requesting for over twenty years.

Production notes

The post-credits scene was filmed in Korea with Korean cast on-location at Seoul exterior sets. Capcom Korea's production team handled the shoot, with Nakanishi providing remote direction.

The scene was added very late in development — internal sources indicate the post-credits scene was finalized only weeks before launch. The decision to commit to a Korean protagonist for RE10 was made during RE9's final post-production.

What this sets up for RE10

Korean BSAA field commander protagonist. Boston as primary plot location. Grace and Sherry Birkin as returning characters. Internal BSAA faction war. Continuation of RE9's institutional corruption plot.

The Korean protagonist will likely also operate in Korea (some chapters set in Seoul or other Korean cities). The franchise is finally giving Korean fans the localized storyline.

Why most theorists got the protagonist wrong

Pre-post-credits, fan speculation focused on either Grace returning as RE10 lead or a returning legacy character (Chris, Jill, Claire). The actual answer — entirely new Korean character — was unexpected by most prediction markets.

This is good. Capcom surprised the audience. The franchise needed a new face. The Korean specificity is meaningful both for representation and for narrative novelty.

My take

The post-credits scene is the most consequential brief cutscene in RE history. Under one minute that establishes the next four years of franchise direction. If you closed the credits without watching, replay the ending. This scene is required.
