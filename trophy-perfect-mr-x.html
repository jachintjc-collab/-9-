Perfect Mr. X — escape Mr. X chase without being struck once

Complete the chapter 6 Mr. X pursuit sequence without taking any hit from Mr. X. Other damage during the chapter doesn't disqualify; only Mr. X-specific contact damage counts.

My take:Perfect Mr. X rewards specific routing mastery. The pursuit isn't actually that hard once you know the route.

What disqualifies

Mr. X strikes Grace with any of his attack moves. Direct contact damage from his patrol movements. Mr. X-triggered environmental hazards (none in chapter 6 fortunately).

Cerberus attacks during chapter 6: don't count against Perfect Mr. X. Only the Tyrant unit matters.

The Perfect Mr. X route

Archive room exit → western corridor (Mr. X is in main lobby) → main lobby (he's left already if timed correctly) → eastern stairwell. Don't backtrack. Don't enter chief's office (his secondary patrol). Don't take the basement shortcut (Cerberus trigger).

The route takes 3-4 minutes of focused play. Practice attempts add to the time.

Save management

Save at the chapter start. Reload immediately on any Mr. X contact. Don't try to recover — the trophy is binary.

Insanity Perfect Mr. X

Insanity Mr. X moves faster and patrols more aggressively. Perfect on Insanity is significantly harder than Standard. Standard recommended for first completion.

Korean perfect routing

Korean speedrun community has documented Perfect Mr. X with frame-precise route timing. The Korean route includes one specific positioning trick (using the archive room desk as obstacle) not in English-language guides.

My take

Perfect Mr. X is achievable in 5-10 attempts for engaged players. Don't pursue on Insanity unless you've mastered Standard first. The trophy rewards memorization of patrol patterns more than reflex skill.
