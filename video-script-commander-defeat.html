Video Script

Commander X1 defeat — chapter 7 dialogue + resolution scene

Commander X1's defeat in chapter 7 includes a three-minute resolution cutscene that recontextualizes the fight. The Commander isn't simply defeated — he chooses to disengage. The scene tells you the franchise has more Master Plagas integration storylines to tell.

My take:Commander X1's defeat scene is the franchise's first boss resolution that ends in dialogue rather than death. RE10 needs to develop this further. Commander X1's narrative isn't finished.

The full transcript

[ARK lower facility. After the Commander X1 boss fight (phase 3 successful completion). Commander X1 stands wounded but functional. He's down to roughly 5% health bar. Grace's weapon is still drawn.]

GRACE: "Don't move."

COMMANDER X1 (slight breathing labor, dignified): "I have stopped. Your weapon is unnecessary."

GRACE: "Then surrender."

COMMANDER X1: "I will not."

GRACE: "I'll kill you."

COMMANDER X1: "You will. But you should know what you are killing before you do."

[Beat. Grace doesn't lower her weapon but holds fire.]

GRACE: "Talk fast."

COMMANDER X1: "I am the eighty-third Master Plagas integration attempt. I am the third successful integration. The first two are dead — one by suicide, one in operational deployment. I am the only living successful integration."

GRACE: "Then there will be more attempts."

COMMANDER X1: "Sixty-two are scheduled in the next eighteen months. The funding is in place. The candidates have been selected."

GRACE: "Who's funding it?"

COMMANDER X1: "The same BSAA elements that funded Vivien's research."

[Grace processes. The connection to Father Riley's reveal lands.]

COMMANDER X1: "I was a willing volunteer. I chose this integration. The successors will not have my level of consent — most will be coerced or deceived. The program will produce dozens of failed integrations. Each failed integration is a death by extended torture."

GRACE: "Why are you telling me this?"

COMMANDER X1: "Because I am dying soon regardless. The integration is degrading. I have weeks, not months. If my death is to mean something, the program should not continue past me."

GRACE: "You want me to expose it."

COMMANDER X1: "I want you to stop it. Expose it if you must, but stop it. The candidates are people, Grace. Eighty-three lives have been wasted on this program already. I am the most successful outcome and even I am dying."

[Pause.]

GRACE: "I'll add it to what I'm publishing."

COMMANDER X1: "Then my death has meaning."

[Grace lowers her weapon. Commander X1 sits on a containment crate. He breathes deeply, in obvious physical decline.]

COMMANDER X1: "Go. Eveline is in the next chamber. I have no further opposition to offer you."

GRACE: "Will you survive the wait?"

COMMANDER X1: "Not long. But I will see this finished."

[Grace continues toward the ARK core. Commander X1 watches her leave. Camera lingers on him briefly as he closes his eyes.]

What the scene establishes

Commander X1 was a willing volunteer in the Master Plagas integration program. He's the third successful integration ever. The program continues with sixty-two scheduled candidates. BSAA-affiliated elements are funding the integration program. The successor candidates will mostly not be willing volunteers.

This is the chapter 7 institutional reveal. The BSAA-funded research isn't limited to Vivien's gene therapy work — it extends to Master Plagas integration. The institutional corruption is broader than chapter 5 implied.

The sixty-two candidates thread for RE10

Sixty-two scheduled Master Plagas integration candidates is a major RE10 thread. Some will be willing volunteers (like Commander X1). Most will be coerced. The integration program is the kind of moral atrocity that demands narrative consequences.

RE10 has the option to feature: rescue mission for coerced candidates, antagonist who's a successful integration like Commander X1, investigation into who specifically is running the program at BSAA.

Voice work

Commander X1 is voiced by Steve Blum (longtime industry presence, RE Code Veronica's Steve Burnside historically). His vocal delivery on the integration program reveal is the scene's anchor. Blum plays Commander X1 as dignified, dying, and morally serious — not as defeated boss villain.

Cissy Jones (Grace) plays the scene with professional listening. She doesn't escalate when she hears the BSAA connection; she absorbs and continues. The control is the character work.

Korean dub notes

Commander X1's Korean VA, 김기현 (also Father Riley), brought specific gravity to the integration program reveal. Korean Naver discussion identified this as one of the most ethically complex moments in the Korean translation. The Korean rendering of "I want you to stop it" — "이걸 멈춰 주세요" — preserved Commander X1's exhausted dignity.

Production notes

This scene was a late addition during development. Earlier drafts ended Commander X1's boss fight with traditional death cutscene. The dialogue resolution was added after internal feedback that Commander X1's character had more potential than a single boss fight allowed.

The scene's lighting (low, single-source warm) was specifically designed to make Commander X1 read as dignified rather than menacing. The fight lighting was harsh and clinical; the resolution lighting was warm and respectful.

What this sets up for RE10

The Master Plagas integration program. The BSAA institutional connection. The sixty-two candidates. The dying Commander X1 (he'll be dead before RE10 but his existence will be referenced).

Korean fan theory threads expect RE10 to feature a Commander X1 successor as antagonist — different motivations, different alliance, but similar Master Plagas integration capability.

My take

Commander X1's defeat scene is the franchise's first boss resolution that ends with dialogue rather than death. The character earned this treatment through the fight's design quality. If you respected the boss fight, you'll respect the resolution. Watch it.
