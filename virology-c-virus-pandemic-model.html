C-virus pandemic model — what the math says

The C-virus first showed up in RE6, then got rebooted as background lore in RE9. I want to walk through whether the pandemic model actually makes biological sense, or whether it's just scary numbers Capcom threw at a wall.

My take:RE6's C-virus had the worst science of any RE virus. RE9 quietly retconned the worst parts and most reviewers didn't notice.

The short version

The C-virus was originally written as a chimera — part Progenitor, part T, part G, with a Las Plagas dominance element bolted on for the J'avo transformation. RE9 ignores the chimera angle and treats C-virus as a Progenitor branch line. That's a quiet retcon, but it's the right call.

If you stack the original RE6 codex against RE9 background terminals, you'll see four C-virus claims that no longer hold: the 100% mortality figure, the airborne dispersal radius, the "B.O.W. inheritance" mechanic, and the cross-species replication speed. All four are gone. I think Capcom realized the original numbers were embarrassing if you sat down with a pen.

What actual virology says about a Progenitor-style pathogen

Progenitor in the canon is a single-strand RNA virus with retroviral integration. Real-world parallels: HIV uses similar machinery (reverse transcriptase + integrase). The plausibility cliff is the cross-species infectivity. HIV took roughly 50 years to jump from primates to humans efficiently. Progenitor in canon jumps host within 24 hours. That's the number Capcom never explains.

My read: the in-universe handwave is that Progenitor evolved alongside the founder cyanobacterium in the Sussex Tower flower, which gave it a kind of universal entry mechanism. It's nonsense but it's internally consistent nonsense, and that's the bar for RE.

The pandemic model itself

RE9's Vivien Davies codex talks about a notional R0 of 12+ for engineered C-variants. For comparison: measles is 12-18, the most contagious natural pathogen we know. Engineered C-virus matching measles transmissibility is plausible if you assume Capcom's "aerosol mutation" plot point. Plausible at the magazine-cover level. Not plausible at the molecular-biology level.

The reason: respiratory transmission requires specific receptor binding (ACE2 for SARS-CoV-2, hemagglutinin for influenza). Capcom's lore never specifies the receptor. If you press the question, the answer collapses.

Where RE9 quietly fixed RE6's mistakes

RE9 background lore drops three things RE6 had:

The chimera origin story (RE6: Carla Radames fused T, G, and Plagas; RE9 doesn't reference this). The instant-J'avo transformation (RE6: minutes; RE9: weeks of progressive mutation, much more biologically sensible). The "creature can pass infection through DNA exchange" mechanic (gone entirely).

These quiet fixes are why I rank RE9's biology writing above RE6 even though RE6 had more screen time for the C-virus. Less ambition, more rigor.

Korean RE community on this

Naver Café and 디씨 RE 갤러리 threads on RE9 lore tend to skip C-virus discussion. Korean readers are still arguing about Mia Winters and Eveline, which makes sense given those are the immediate plot. The C-virus is treated as background-decoration. I think that's correct — it's not load-bearing for RE9's main story.

Where it gets interesting is when Korean reviewers compare C-virus pandemic stakes to recent real-world events. There's a clear post-2020 sensitivity that wasn't there for RE6 reviews. Capcom seems to have read the room — the in-game C-virus content is restrained.

Verdict

The C-virus pandemic model in RE9 is the most credible version of this concept the series has ever had. That's a low bar (RE6 was a mess), but RE9 cleared it.

If you're a virology nerd, the receptor question is the rabbit hole that'll bother you. Everyone else, suspension of disbelief works fine. Recommended reading after the playthrough, not during.
