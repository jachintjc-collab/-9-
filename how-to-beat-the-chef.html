How to Beat The Chef in Resident Evil Requiem (RE9)

PUBLISHED MAY 2026 · 5 MIN READ · DIFFICULTY: ★ EASY · SPOILERS AHEAD
⚠ SPOILER LEVEL: LOW

The Chef is an optional mini-boss in the Care Center Kitchen — a mutated cook wielding a cleaver, guarding a small cache of herbs and ammunition. This guide covers the Pantry Door Key requirement, combat tactics, and whether the fight is worth your time on different run types.

ℹ This is an optional boss — The Chef can be entirely skipped during a normal playthrough. He is encountered only if you choose to investigate the Care Center Kitchen and obtain the Pantry Door Key. Skipping costs you several useful items including herbs and an ammunition cache, but does not block story progression.

Boss overview: Who is The Chef?

The Chef is an optional mini-boss tucked away in the Care Center Kitchen, encountered early in Grace's section. He's described as a large, virus-mutated cook wielding a massive cleaver, and he guards a small cache of useful items including herbs and ammunition.

Mechanically, he's a melee-focused encounter — fast cleaver swings at close range, very limited movement options for Grace in a tight kitchen environment, and no ranged attacks. The risk-reward calculation is straightforward: spend ammo and possibly take some damage in exchange for crafting supplies and a side note that fills out the Care Center's narrative.

How to access the encounter

The Chef is sealed behind a locked door in the Care Center Kitchen. To trigger the encounter, you must first obtain the Pantry Door Key, which is hidden elsewhere in the Care Center.

If you're playing a completionist run, the Pantry Door Key is part of the Care Center 100% checklist. If you're speedrunning, this is one of the first items to skip — the time cost of finding the key and clearing the encounter outweighs the resource gain.

Combat strategy

The Chef is a straightforward melee fight in a confined space. The kitchen layout limits movement, but the enemy has no ranged options, so positioning is the entire fight.

// STEP 1 — STAY ON THE OPPOSITE SIDE OF THE PREP ISLAND

The kitchen has a central prep island that acts as a natural barrier. Use it. Keep the island between you and The Chef, force him to circle, and fire shots when he commits to one direction.

// STEP 2 — TARGET THE HEAD

Like most mutated enemies in Resident Evil: Requiem, headshots are the most efficient damage type. Handgun headshots stagger him; shotgun blasts at the head stagger and drop chunks of his health per shot.

// STEP 3 — DODGE THE CLEAVER LUNGE

The Chef's primary attack is a forward cleaver lunge — well-telegraphed but very fast. When you see the wind-up animation, dodge sideways, not backward. Backward dodges keep you in his hit cone.

// AMMO RECOMMENDATIONS

Treat this fight as practice for the Care Center's later encounters. Don't burn premium ammunition like Requiem bullets — handgun rounds and standard shotgun shells are sufficient. Total ammo expenditure should be 8-12 rounds across the fight.

Is The Chef worth fighting?

For a first-time playthrough, yes — the encounter is narratively complete (it adds context to the Care Center's collapse) and the herb cache inside the pantry is meaningful for the Plant 43 fight coming up later.

For a Platinum run, yes — completing all optional encounters contributes to the bestiary and supports the side-content trophies.

For a sub-4-hour speedrun, no — skip it. The 6-8 minutes you save are more valuable than the herbs you'd gain.

Rewards and what comes next

Defeating The Chef grants:

  • Access to the pantry with herbs (typically 2-3 green herbs and 1 red herb)
  • Ammunition cache (handgun and shotgun rounds)
  • Narrative document explaining the Care Center kitchen's role in the outbreak timeline
  • Contribution to the optional-encounter bestiary count

The herbs are particularly useful — by stockpiling green and red herbs through the Care Center, you can craft enhanced healing items for the Water Treatment Facility encounter with The Girl, where damage is harder to avoid.
