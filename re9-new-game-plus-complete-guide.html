RE9 New Game Plus complete guide — what carries, what resets, optimal NG+ strategy

RE9's New Game Plus mode inherits weapons, charms, currency, and weapon attachments from your completion save. Collectibles, documents, and audio logs reset. This complete guide covers inheritance rules, optimal NG+ strategies for different goals (speedrun, trophy completion, story exploration), and the specific NG+ exclusive content most players never see.

My take:NG+ is RE9's most-rewarding second-playthrough mode. Don't skip the inherited weapons; don't try to NG+ with vanilla loadout. The mode is designed around inheritance.

What carries to NG+ from your completion save

Weapons: all unlocked weapons carry forward, including Hand Cannon Prototype (Insanity completion unlock) and Mortal Edge knife (Insanity completion unlock). Weapon attachments and upgrades also carry.

Charms: all 18 charms carry, including charm slots already filled. Active charm loadout carries to NG+.

Currency: full currency balance carries. Merchants in NG+ have inflated prices to balance for inherited currency, but the inflation is roughly 30% — not enough to negate inheritance value.

Weapon attachments: all purchased and looted attachments carry. Stack mods (e.g., extended magazine, recoil compensator) carry.

What resets in NG+

Story progress: full reset. NG+ starts from chapter 1 opening cinematic.

Documents: all 89 documents reset. You must re-read for the Detective trophy if not earned previously.

Audio logs: all 47 audio logs reset. You must re-listen for the Lorekeeper trophy if not earned previously.

Mr. Raccoon plushies: all 25 reset. Re-collection required for All Raccoons trophy if not previously earned.

Treasures: all 18 reset. Re-collection required for Treasure Hunter trophy if not previously earned.

Dialogue choice history: all dialogue branches reset. Earn different endings in NG+ runs.

NG+ exclusive content

Dialogue meta-awareness: chapter 1 Grace dialogue includes meta-recognition that player has been here before. Specific lines change between first playthrough and NG+. Korean Naver community first documented this in March 2026.

Chapter 5 Vivien NG+ recognition: Vivien's chapter 5 dialogue includes a line about Grace returning. The implication is that Vivien somehow recognizes Grace's prior experience. Reading this as metafiction or actual narrative element is up to player interpretation.

Chapter 7 Leon NG+ acknowledgment: Leon's chapter 7 farewell dialogue includes acknowledgment of player's previous completion. Smaller meta touch but emotionally rewarding for engaged players.

Rocket Launcher: NG+ unlocks the Rocket Launcher in inventory at chapter 1 start. 5 total rockets per NG+ playthrough.

Optimal NG+ strategies by goal

Speedrun goal: target sub-4-hour completion. NG+ with cutscene skip + optimal routing achievable. See speedrun-guide-sub-4-hours for detailed route. Trophy reward: Speedrunner.

Trophy completion goal: NG+ enables most challenging trophies. True Survivor requires 100% Insanity NG+. Knife Only Insanity requires Mortal Edge inheritance. Pacifist requires NG+ stealth knowledge.

Story exploration goal: NG+ allows exploring alternative endings without first-playthrough story commitment. Use save scumming at chapter 8 final decision to access multiple endings efficiently.

Combat practice goal: NG+ with all weapons available lets you experiment with builds without first-playthrough difficulty pressure.

Recommended NG+ playthrough order

First NG+: pursue trophy completion goals you missed in first playthrough. Use inherited equipment to clean up trophy backlog efficiently. Target trophies: Detective, Lorekeeper, All Charms, All Raccoons, Mosaic.

Second NG+ (NG++): pursue alternative endings. Use save scumming at branch points. Target endings: Grace true ending, Leon true ending, Father Riley confession ending, Secret ending (if eligible).

Third NG+ (NG+++): pursue Insanity for ultimate completion. Use Hand Cannon and Mortal Edge knife inherited from previous Insanity completion if available. Target: True Survivor trophy.

Charm reallocation strategy in NG+

Standard NG+ playthrough recommended loadout: Endurance + Marksman's Eye + Quickstep. Optimized for trophy hunting and exploration.

Knife-only NG+ playthrough: Mortal Edge + Knife Edge + Quickstep. Note that Knife Edge stacks with Mortal Edge base damage; combined damage is roughly 3.4x base knife damage on critical hits.

No-heal NG+ playthrough: Endurance + Phoenix Down + Survivor's Will. Damage mitigation focus.

Speedrun NG+ playthrough: Quickstep + Marksman's Eye + Magnetic Hand. Movement and combat efficiency.

NG+ chapter pacing

Chapter 1: 15-25 minutes with cutscene skip. Skip Brass Whistle collection if already obtained.

Chapter 2: 20-30 minutes. Skip Mr. Raccoon collection unless completionist goal.

Chapter 3: 25-35 minutes. Boss encounters dramatically easier with Hand Cannon (1-2 rounds to defeat Vivien Plant 43).

Chapter 4: 20-30 minutes with forest glitch + cutscene skip.

Chapter 5: 25-35 minutes. Quartz puzzle remains problematic regardless of NG+ status.

Chapter 6: 45-60 minutes. Mr. X chase still requires precise routing.

Chapter 7: 35-50 minutes. Commander X1 boss easier with Hand Cannon. Skip dialogue cutscenes for trophy-focused runs; keep them for story exploration runs.

Chapter 8: 35-55 minutes. Eveline final boss easier with Hand Cannon. Final ending dialogue depends on prior chapter 6 archive completion.

NG+ specific trophies

NG+ Master: complete one NG+ playthrough. The simplest NG+ trophy.

Speed Demon: complete in under 6 hours. NG+ casual speedrun.

Speedrunner: complete in under 4 hours. NG+ optimized speedrun. Korean Naver routing required.

True Survivor: 100% on Insanity. Requires NG+ Insanity playthrough.

Grace True Ending: requires specific dialogue + chapter 6 archive reading. NG+ doesn't carry archive read status, so re-read required.

DCInside RE gallery has charm loadout discussion threads specific to NG+ play styles. The Korean community has been particularly engaged with NG+ optimization since launch.

Common NG+ mistakes

Selling inherited weapons: don't do this. The currency value is low; the weapon retention value is high.

Skipping dialogue cutscenes for first NG+: don't do this. NG+ meta-aware dialogue is meaningful content. Skip cutscenes on third or fourth playthrough, not second.

Attempting Insanity NG+ as first NG+: don't do this. Build NG+ familiarity on Casual or Standard first.

Not enabling cloud backup: critical mistake. Save corruption on long NG+ runs destroys hours of progress.

Final NG+ recommendation

If you completed RE9 first playthrough on Standard: do first NG+ on Hardcore to bridge difficulty before attempting Insanity. The inheritance helps; difficulty step matters.

If you completed first playthrough on Insanity: NG+ on Standard for relaxed trophy hunting. Save Insanity NG+ for True Survivor pursuit specifically.

If you completed on Casual: NG+ on Standard. Build skills before attempting harder modes.

The franchise's most-rewarding second playthrough mode. Use it intentionally.
