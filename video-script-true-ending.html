RE9 true ending — full transcript + what it means for RE10

RE9's true ending unlocks after specific chapter 6 and chapter 8 conditions. It adds about three minutes of additional content past the standard ending. The true ending changes the franchise's trajectory more significantly than the standard ending implies.

My take:The true ending is required watching for anyone planning to play RE10. Most players miss it because the unlock conditions aren't obvious. Replay if you didn't see it.

The true ending unlock conditions

Two requirements: read all chapter 6 archive documents (specifically the Sherry Birkin involvement files), and decline Eveline's final dialogue option in chapter 8 (the third dialogue choice, "I don't accept this," rather than the default "I had to" or the alternative "I'm sorry too").

Most players miss the true ending because the chapter 6 archives are easy to skip and the third dialogue option requires specific positioning. The Korean speedrun community has documented the unlock conditions; the English wiki coverage is less complete.

The full true ending transcript

[Standard ending scene plays: Grace exits the ARK facility. Dawn. Distant sirens.]

[Where standard ending cuts to credits, true ending continues.]

[Grace's phone rings. Caller ID: "S. Birkin"]

[Grace looks at the phone. Hesitates. Answers.]

GRACE: "Birkin."

SHERRY (phone): "Grace. I know what you found."

GRACE: "Then you know what I'm going to do about it."

SHERRY: "I do. That's why I'm calling."

[Beat.]

SHERRY: "There are people inside BSAA who would prefer the cover-up holds. People who were there twenty-three years ago. They will not let you publish."

GRACE: "I'm publishing."

SHERRY: "I know. I'm calling to offer you what protection I can. And to ask one thing."

GRACE: "What?"

SHERRY: "When you publish, include my involvement in the reconstruction. Don't protect me from what I helped enable."

[Pause. Grace processes this.]

GRACE: "You knew what BSAA did to my brother's case."

SHERRY: "I learned in 2010, during the reconstruction. By then it was too late to undo. I should have come to you. I didn't."

GRACE: "Why now?"

SHERRY: "Because someone has to start telling the truth. And you started."

[Beat. Grace looks toward the distant horizon.]

GRACE: "I'll include your name. All of it."

SHERRY: "Thank you, Grace."

[Call ends. Grace stands alone. Cut to interior of a BSAA conference room. Multiple unfamiliar officials sit at a table. The ranking officer speaks.]

BSAA OFFICIAL (unknown): "Ashcroft is going to publish."

SECOND OFFICIAL: "Birkin?"

BSAA OFFICIAL: "Birkin is with her now."

[Beat.]

SECOND OFFICIAL: "Activate the contingency."

[Cut to black. Title card: RESIDENT EVIL — TO BE CONTINUED]

What the true ending establishes

Sherry Birkin is now an ally character for Grace (and presumably for RE10). The BSAA contains a faction that's hostile to Grace's investigation. The "contingency" mentioned by the official is unspecified but ominous. The franchise has set up RE10 as a direct continuation of RE9's institutional plot.

The "TO BE CONTINUED" title card is the franchise's most explicit RE10 confirmation. Capcom is committing to RE10 happening and being a continuation of RE9's plot threads.

Why the dialogue choice matters

The "I don't accept this" option in chapter 8 demonstrates Grace's refusal to let the situation be resolved with Eveline's death. This refusal is what triggers Sherry's confidence — she knows Grace won't drop the investigation. Without this refusal, Grace appears to accept resolution; Sherry's offer of alliance wouldn't make sense.

The Korean speedrun community documented this dependency. Most English-language guides don't explain why the dialogue choice matters; they just list it as the unlock condition.

Voice work in the true ending

Sherry Birkin is voiced by Karen Strassman (returning from RE6). The voice work has substantially deepened from her RE6 performance — Sherry is now an adult professional in tone, no longer the young woman of her last appearance.

The two BSAA officials at the table are uncredited. Their performances are deliberately flat-affect — generic institutional voices. The faceless authority framing is part of the threat setup for RE10.

Korean dub notes

Sherry's Korean VA, 김지영, recorded the true ending phone scene specifically. The Korean rendering of "I'll include your name. All of it." — "당신 이름까지 다 적을 거예요. 전부" — got specific praise on Naver discussion threads as one of the most affecting line reads in the game.

Production notes

The true ending was added late in development (according to a Nakanishi interview at GDC 2026). The original RE9 ending was just the standard version. The decision to add the true ending came from internal narrative team feedback that the chapter 6 Sherry Birkin reference needed payoff in the same game rather than waiting for RE10.

The "TO BE CONTINUED" title card was approved at the highest Capcom level. Including it required formal commitment to RE10 development.

What this means for RE10

RE10 will feature Grace Ashcroft as returning character (probably co-protagonist, not lead). Sherry Birkin will be involved as ally. The internal BSAA faction will be the antagonist institution. The "contingency" is the inciting event for RE10's plot.

The Korean fan theory consensus aligns with this read. The true ending essentially outlines RE10's premise.

My take

The true ending is essential watching. The "TO BE CONTINUED" framing changes how RE9 itself reads — knowing RE10 will continue the institutional thread makes RE9's setup work feel deliberate rather than open-ended. If you missed the true ending, replay specifically to see it. The investment is worth it.
