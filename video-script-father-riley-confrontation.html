Video Script

Father Riley confrontation — chapter 5 manor scene

Father Riley's role in the Wrenwood cover-up gets revealed in chapter 5, during the Rhodes Hill manor sequence. The five-minute confrontation cutscene reframes Riley from helpful local clergy to compromised institutional figure. The reveal is RE9's biggest character pivot.

My take:Father Riley's chapter 5 reveal is the franchise's best institutional-corruption character beat. Lance Henriksen's performance is the best vocal performance in the game. Required watching.

The full transcript

[Grace enters the Rhodes Hill manor library. Father Riley is already there, seated in an armchair, holding a glass of wine. The fire is lit. The atmosphere reads as deliberately staged.]

FATHER RILEY: "Sit down, Grace. We need to talk."

GRACE: "How did you know I'd come here?"

FATHER RILEY: "Vivien's records. She left a backup with me. Standard practice — her legal counsel and her confessor."

GRACE: "You're her confessor."

FATHER RILEY: "For seventeen years. Since before the original Care Center research."

[Grace processes this. Slowly sits in the chair opposite Riley.]

GRACE: "You knew about the outbreak before it happened."

FATHER RILEY: "I knew Vivien was working with material she wasn't qualified to handle. I knew she was cutting corners on containment. I knew local authorities were not investigating her properly."

GRACE: "And you did nothing."

FATHER RILEY: "I'm a priest, Grace. Information shared in confession is not mine to share with others."

GRACE: "Three investigators died. People died in the outbreak. You could have warned them."

FATHER RILEY: "And I would have violated the seal of confession. The Church's position on this is clear, even when the outcomes are catastrophic."

[Pause. Henriksen's vocal delivery in this scene is the production's centerpiece.]

FATHER RILEY: "I have not slept properly in three years. I have prayed. I have asked for guidance. I have been told that the choice I made — to honor the seal — was correct. I do not believe that. I cannot tell you what I do believe. But I know that what I chose has weight I will carry for the rest of my life."

[Beat.]

GRACE: "Why are you telling me this now?"

FATHER RILEY: "Because Vivien is dead. Her confession is no longer privileged. I can speak now without violating the seal."

GRACE: "You waited for her to die."

FATHER RILEY: "I waited for her to confess to you publicly, which she did before her death. That's what allows me to speak now. The seal protects living penitents."

GRACE: "So tell me what you know."

FATHER RILEY: "BSAA has people in Wrenwood. They've been here longer than Vivien. The Care Center research was their request, not Vivien's initiative."

[Grace stops. This is the major reveal.]

GRACE: "What?"

FATHER RILEY: "Vivien thought she was working independently. She wasn't. BSAA was funding the research through three shell corporations. They wanted the gene therapy work. The bioweapon applications were... let's say not opposed."

GRACE: "Names. Who at BSAA."

FATHER RILEY: "I have names. I'll give them to you. But Grace — once I do, you're at risk. The men I'll name killed three investigators already. They'll kill more."

GRACE: "Give me the names, Father."

[Riley reaches into his cassock. Produces a sealed envelope. Hands it to Grace.]

FATHER RILEY: "May God forgive me for breaking the seal. And may He forgive you for what you'll do with this information."

[Grace takes the envelope. The chapter ends shortly after.]

What the scene establishes

Father Riley knew about the Care Center research from Vivien's confessions over seventeen years. He withheld warning because of confessional privilege. He believes his choice was wrong but believes he had no theological alternative. BSAA itself is implicated in the Care Center research — not just failing to prevent it.

The BSAA involvement reveal is RE9's biggest plot turn. The institutional cover-up Grace has been investigating isn't just BSAA failing to catch a rogue researcher — it's BSAA actively having sponsored the research that became the outbreak.

The confessional ethics

The scene presents the confessional seal as genuine ethical constraint rather than convenient plot device. Father Riley doesn't break the seal opportunistically; he waits until Vivien's death and public confession make her sins no longer privileged.

This is unusually careful religious ethics for a video game. Most games treat clergy characters as either fully sympathetic or fully corrupt. Father Riley is bound by genuine theological obligations he doesn't agree with but won't violate.

Lance Henriksen's performance

The "I have not slept properly in three years" monologue is the best individual vocal performance in RE9. Henriksen plays Father Riley as a man whose moral certainty has been ruined by following his own rules. The exhaustion in the vocal delivery is the performance's signature.

This is the scene that pays off Capcom's casting of Henriksen specifically. A less experienced vocal performer would have either oversold the moral conflict or underplayed it. Henriksen finds the precise middle.

Korean dub notes

Father Riley's Korean VA, 김기현, faced the most difficult vocal challenge of the Korean dub. The Korean rendering of the "I have not slept properly in three years" monologue — "지난 삼 년간 잠을 제대로 잔 적이 없습니다" — required matching Henriksen's specific emotional cadence.

Korean Naver discussion ranked this scene as the strongest Korean dub moment in the game. Korean readers responded particularly to the confessional ethics framing.

Production notes

This scene took the longest vocal recording session of any RE9 cutscene. Henriksen reportedly did 22 takes of the "I have not slept properly" monologue before Nakanishi was satisfied. The director's notes emphasized that Riley needed to read as exhausted, not as performatively guilty.

The blocking (Riley seated, Grace eventually sits opposite, both staying seated through the conversation) was specifically directed to read as confessional itself — a quiet conversation in private space rather than a tense confrontation.

The envelope contents

The names in the envelope are referenced but not shown in this scene. The chapter 6 RPD archive scene references the names indirectly (BSAA personnel identified through Grace's archive research). The full names appear in the true ending epilogue.

Korean speedrun community documented that reading the envelope contents in chapter 5 (it's accessible in inventory after this scene) provides additional context for chapter 6 dialogue. Most English-language guides don't note this.

What this sets up

The BSAA institutional plot for the rest of RE9 and into RE10. Grace's risk level escalates dramatically after this scene. The Sherry Birkin allyship in the true ending makes sense because Grace now knows BSAA has internal hostile factions.

My take

The Father Riley confrontation is the chapter 5 must-watch cutscene. Henriksen's performance alone justifies the chapter. The institutional reveal changes RE9's entire premise. If you skipped this cutscene, you don't have the game Capcom made.
