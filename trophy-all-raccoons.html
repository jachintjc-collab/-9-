All Mr. Raccoon plushies (25 of them) — where Capcom hid them this time

Twenty-five Mr. Raccoon plushies scattered across all four areas. Like every previous RE game with Mr. Raccoon collection, this trophy is the franchise's most universally hated collectible. Here's the actual map.

My take:Mr. Raccoon is the worst RE collectible tradition. Capcom should retire it. Until they do, here's where the 25 live.

Distribution by area

Wrenwood: 7 plushies. Rhodes Hill: 8 plushies. Raccoon City: 5 plushies (the RPD chapter has fewer despite the name — Capcom's joke). ARK: 5 plushies, two of which are in the final boss area (don't trigger the boss before collecting).

The four chapter-locked plushies

Wrenwood hotel basement (chapter 1 only — sealed after chapter 2 transition). Rhodes Hill stable rooftop (chapter 5 only — area collapses end of chapter). Raccoon City library stack 7B (chapter 6 only — the Mr. X chase route doesn't let you backtrack here). ARK biolab control room (chapter 8 only — sealed by the final boss trigger).

Missing any of these four requires NG+ run to recover. The trophy doesn't carry across saves cleanly — NG+ restarts Mr. Raccoon counter.

The single most-missed plushy

Wrenwood hotel hidden under the receptionist's desk. Requires interaction with the desk drawer from behind, which most players never do because the receptionist's NPC dialogue points you the other direction. Discovery rate per Capcom telemetry (released at GDC 2026): 4%.

Insanity difference

None for plushy locations. But Insanity respawn timing on some plushy-adjacent enemy encounters can make collection harder. The Rhodes Hill stable rooftop on Insanity has a Plant 43 spawn that wasn't there on Standard — bring W870 Police.

Korean fan community map

The Korean Naver RE9 community published a complete annotated Mr. Raccoon map in March 2026. It's more accurate than the official strategy guide map (which has two incorrect locations). Search "RE9 라쿤 인형 위치" on Naver for the current version.

My take

Mr. Raccoon trophy is the franchise's tax for series fans. Pay it once, never again. Use a chapter-by-chapter checklist (the Korean Naver map is the cleanest). Don't try to remember the locations between sessions — the count won't match your memory.
