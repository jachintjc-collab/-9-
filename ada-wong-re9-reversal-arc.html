The pattern across 21 years: Ada is consistently helpful to Leon but never emotionally available. She owes him her life multiple times but refuses to admit it. RE9's Secret Ending breaks this pattern definitively.

Leon Secret Ending — full cutscene breakdown

▶ REVEAL: Ada Wong in Leon Secret Ending

Setup: Leon Secret Ending requires NG+ Standard+ difficulty, picking up Ada's beacon in the ARK Server Room (only spawns after Grace True is in the save), and choosing "Preserve Sample."

The reveal (~6 minutes): Leon finds Ada Wong's tracker beacon embedded in a server rack. He follows the signal to a remote dock 30km from ARK. Ada is there; bleeding out from a stab wound, slumped against a shipping container.

Key dialogue (verbatim):

  • Leon: "Ada. What the hell happened?"
  • Ada: "They... they finally figured out which side I was on. Should have... should have told you sooner."
  • Leon: "Hold on. Stay with me."
  • Ada: "Thank you, Leon. For everything."

The first "Thank you": This is the first time in 21 years of RE canon (RE2 1998 → present) that Ada has explicitly thanked Leon on-screen. Fans have noted this is the largest emotional pivot in the Leon/Ada arc ever filmed.

The ending shot: Leon carries Ada to a safehouse. Final frame: Leon bandaging her shoulder, Ada's hand resting on his arm. Fade to black. No mid-credits scene.

Why the first "thank you" matters

In 21 years of canon (RE2 1998 → RE9 2026), Ada Wong has never said the words "thank you, Leon" on-screen. She's thanked him implicitly through actions. leaving him weapons, warning him of traps, saving Helena off-screen — but never with that exact phrase.

RE9's Secret Ending uses this deliberately. The dialogue ("Thank you, Leon. For everything") is positioned as the moment Ada's character mask finally drops. Director Koshi Nakanishi confirmed in a Famitsu Q&A that this scene was "the most rewritten dialogue in the game; 17 drafts before final."

Why it matters for the franchise: Ada's emotional inaccessibility has been a defining trait. By breaking it now, Capcom signals a new era for the Leon/Ada arc. possibly leading to direct partnership in RE10 or a Leon-focused DLC.

Who stabbed Ada? (data-mined answer)

▶ Who stabbed Ada? (data-mined answer)

In-game: Ada doesn't name her attacker. Leon never learns who did it during the cutscene.

Data-mined files (v1.02 patch): Capcom's code includes unused dialogue: "Albert's son. He found me first."

Interpretation: This strongly implies the attacker is Jake Muller (Albert Wesker's son, last seen in RE6 2012). Jake has been off-screen for 14 years — RE9 may be setting up his return as a major antagonist.

Alternative theory: "Albert's son" could also refer to a previously unknown Wesker child. some fans believe Capcom is rebooting Jake's character with a new design.

The Wesker connection rabbit hole

If "Albert's son" refers to Jake Muller, here's what we know about him:

  • Last canonical appearance: RE6 (2012); survives the campaign with Sherry Birkin, goes off-grid
  • Power set: Naturally immune to the C-virus due to his Wesker DNA; valuable as bioweapon research subject
  • Motivation in RE9 (theorized): Could be working with The Connections to access ARK Facility's research
  • Why he'd attack Ada: Ada has historically obstructed Wesker-aligned operations. Jake hunting her would close a 14-year loop

The alternative reading: Capcom is introducing an entirely new Wesker child to set up RE10's main antagonist. The character design from datamined model files shows an unknown male in his late 20s with Wesker's trademark sunglasses and silver hair.

What it sets up for RE10 and beyond

Three concrete narrative hooks from Ada's arc:

  1. Leon-Ada partnership becomes official. The Secret Ending positions them in a safehouse together. RE10 likely opens with them working as a team — not in their cat-and-mouse pattern.
  2. The Wesker bloodline returns. "Albert's son" wounding Ada is the seed for a major antagonist reveal. Either Jake Muller is back or a new Wesker child is being introduced.
  3. Ada's real employer revealed. Ada says "they finally figured out which side I was on". implying she's been double-crossing someone for years. RE10 will likely reveal which organization (BSAA, The Family, or independent).

FAQ, Ada in RE9

Q: Is Ada Wong playable in RE9?
A: No. She appears only in Leon's Secret Ending cutscene (~6 minutes). No playable sections.

Q: How do I unlock Leon's Secret Ending?
A: 3 requirements: (1) complete Grace True Ending in the same save, (2) NG+ Standard difficulty or higher, (3) find Ada's beacon in ARK Server Room (only spawns after Grace True). Then choose "Preserve Sample" in Chunk Round 2. Full unlock conditions in our endings guide.

Q: Does Ada die in this ending?
A: Officially ambiguous. The cutscene ends with Leon bandaging her — she's alive at fade-to-black. Capcom hasn't confirmed if she survives long-term. The data-mined audio includes both a "she dies" track and a "she recovers" track. final canon TBD in RE10.

Q: Why isn't Ada in Grace's campaign?
A: Ada has no canonical connection to Grace. Capcom kept her exclusively in Leon's storyline to preserve the 21-year Leon/Ada arc continuity.

Q: Is this the same Ada from RE4 Remake?
A: Yes. Same voice actress (Jolene Andersen), same character model, continuous timeline. The 14-year gap from RE6 is implied to have been spent in deep cover.

Q: What's the significance of the safehouse location?
A: Datamined files name the safehouse "EAGLE-7". a callback to RE6's Operation EAGLE codename. This suggests Leon is using BSAA resources, which would be a major shift for him (he was previously DSO/USSTRATCOM).

Q: Will Ada return in DLC?
A: Capcom hasn't confirmed Ada in DLC specifically. The "untold Winters chapter" leak focuses on Mia, not Ada. But the Secret Ending hooks are clearly setting up RE10 rather than DLC.
