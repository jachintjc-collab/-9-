RE9 reveal trailer — full transcript + reading the marketing strategy

The first reveal trailer (May 2025) introduced RE9 with one minute of footage and three lines of dialogue. Capcom's restraint was deliberate. The trailer's design tells you how the game wanted to be received.

My take:The reveal trailer is RE9's most quietly effective marketing piece. It works because it doesn't try to sell. The full launch trailer five months later was worse marketing despite being more polished.

The full transcript

[Static. Cold blue title card: R E 9]

[Cut to a rain-blurred window. Distant siren.]

WOMAN'S VOICE: "What does it mean to come home?"

[Static. Brief flash: Raccoon City Police Department logo, weathered.]

GRACE: "I shouldn't be here."

LEON: "None of us should. But we are."

[Music swells — strings, low brass]

VIVIEN: "It wasn't supposed to end like this."

[Final card: RESIDENT EVIL: REQUIEM — 2026]

What the trailer chose not to show

No bosses. No combat. No creature reveals. No location detail. No gameplay footage. The trailer's runtime is 67 seconds and 90% of it is darkness, weather effects, and three voice-over lines.

This was the right marketing call. RE7's first trailer in 2016 did the same thing — minimal footage, atmospheric voice-over, mystery emphasis. That trailer set up RE7 as a return-to-horror after RE6's action-game miss. RE9's reveal trailer follows the same playbook.

Voice cast

Grace Ashcroft is voiced by Cissy Jones (Firewatch, Telltale's The Walking Dead). Her delivery on the single line in the trailer carries the entire emotional weight. Casting Jones was Capcom's strongest VA choice in this generation.

Leon S. Kennedy is voiced by Nick Apostolides reprising the role from RE2 Remake. The continuity matters for series fans — RE4 Remake used the same actor.

Vivien Davies is voiced by Laila Berzins. The trailer line is the only Vivien content most viewers heard for five months until the launch trailer.

Korean dub for the trailer

Capcom Korea released the Korean-dubbed reveal trailer simultaneously with the English version. The Vivien line in Korean — "이렇게 끝날 운명이 아니었어" — was singled out by Naver RE forums as the most affecting line in the trailer. Korean voice work by 박지윤 (the Korean Vivien actress) received specific praise.

Production notes

The trailer was directed by Capcom's internal marketing team rather than Yuya Nakanishi (the game's main director). This creates a subtle tonal mismatch — the trailer feels more like RE7 atmosphere, while the actual game leans closer to RE2 Remake's tactical horror.

Music in the trailer is from the in-game score's main theme, composed by Mitsuhiko Takano (Capcom internal composer, RE Village credit). The string arrangement was custom-scored for the trailer; the in-game version is slightly different.

Reception data

The reveal trailer hit 22M YouTube views in the first week. Comments skewed positive — series fans recognized the RE7-style restraint. Korean and Japanese versions outperformed Western language versions per view-to-comment engagement ratio.

My take

The reveal trailer is the marketing piece I'd show in a film school class on restraint. It told you almost nothing and made you want to know more. The full launch trailer five months later (covered separately) revealed too much and lost the mystery. Capcom should have stretched the restraint longer.
