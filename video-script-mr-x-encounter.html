Mr. X encounter — chapter 6 RPD pursuit scene

Mr. X returns in RE9 chapter 6 during the RPD section. The pursuit isn't a cutscene exactly — it's an extended gameplay sequence with cutscene framing at start and end. The framing scenes are RE9's most direct RE2 Remake callbacks.

My take:Mr. X in RE9 is the chapter 6 high point for RE2 Remake veterans. The pursuit sequence respects the legacy. The framing cutscenes earn the callback.

The opening framing cutscene transcript

[Grace in the RPD basement archive room. She's been reading old files. A heavy metallic footstep echoes from above. She looks up.]

GRACE (radio): "Leon, I have movement on the upper floor."

LEON (radio response): "What kind?"

GRACE: "Heavy. Slow. Single source."

[Pause.]

LEON (radio, controlled urgency): "Grace, get out of the archive room. Now."

GRACE: "What is it?"

LEON: "T-103 unit. Active. Confirmed by acoustic signature. Get out, take the eastern stairwell, do not engage."

[Grace closes the archive folder. Starts moving toward the exit. The pursuit gameplay sequence begins.]

The closing framing cutscene transcript

[After Grace successfully evades Mr. X through the chapter 6 RPD sequence and reaches the eastern stairwell exit. She's breathing hard. The Mr. X footsteps fade in the distance.]

GRACE (radio): "Leon. I'm out."

LEON (radio): "Confirmed. T-103 unit is returning to dormant patrol. You're clear."

GRACE: "How is there an active T-103 unit here? Raccoon City was sterilized in 1998."

LEON: "Active T-103 units have been documented in containment facilities since 2010. BSAA recovered three from old Umbrella storage. They're maintained for biothreat training exercises."

GRACE: "This wasn't a training exercise."

LEON: "No. It wasn't."

[Beat.]

GRACE: "Someone activated this unit specifically because I'm here."

LEON: "Confirmed. Activation came from BSAA central server access. I'm tracing the origin now."

GRACE: "Father Riley said BSAA elements would try to kill me. This is what he meant."

LEON: "This is one of the things he meant. There will be more attempts. Get to the chapter 7 staging area. I'll meet you there."

[Grace pockets the archive file she was reading when interrupted. The scene cuts to her exiting the building.]

What the scene establishes

BSAA maintains active T-103 (Mr. X class) Tyrant units in containment storage. The unit Grace encounters was activated specifically to kill her. The activation came from inside BSAA. The institutional threat that Father Riley warned about (chapter 5) is materializing.

The RPD pursuit isn't just a nostalgia sequence. It's the chapter 6 institutional attempt on Grace's life. The cover story would be "tragic training accident" similar to the three previous investigators.

The RE2 Remake callback

The pursuit gameplay sequence is structured similarly to RE2 Remake's iconic Mr. X chase. Same patrol patterns, similar audio cues, comparable tension structure. Players who experienced RE2 Remake recognize the design language immediately.

Capcom didn't try to do something new with the chase mechanic. The RE2 Remake version is the gold standard; RE9 honors it through faithful recreation rather than reinvention.

Voice work in the framing scenes

Nick Apostolides (Leon) gets some of his strongest material in the chapter 6 radio communications. The controlled urgency in "Grace, get out of the archive room. Now." line is the franchise voice work standard for tactical horror.

Cissy Jones (Grace) plays the chase aftermath with controlled exhaustion. Grace isn't dramatically affected by the close call — this is her job. The professional reading is character work.

Korean dub notes

Leon's Korean VA, 정승욱, handled the chapter 6 radio communications particularly well. Korean Naver discussion praised the urgency in his line readings. The Korean rendering of the chase sequence radio chatter was widely appreciated.

The Mr. X return generated significant Korean nostalgia. RE2 Remake had strong Korean reception in 2019; the Mr. X callback in RE9 was a deliberate appeal to that established Korean fanbase.

Production notes

The Mr. X model in RE9 uses the same base asset as RE2 Remake with current-gen lighting and animation upgrades. The visual continuity was deliberate — Capcom wanted Mr. X to read as the same specific unit (possibly the same Mr. X from RE2 Remake, since RE9 reveals BSAA stored multiple units).

The acoustic signature design (the famous Mr. X footstep audio) was directly imported from RE2 Remake with subtle modernization. The sound design team specifically wanted the footsteps to be identifiable to RE2 Remake players.

What this sets up

The institutional threat is now active. Grace isn't being investigated by BSAA hostiles — she's being killed. The escalation continues through chapter 7 and pays off in the true ending's "contingency" reference.

The Mr. X unit itself isn't destroyed in chapter 6 — it returns to dormant patrol after losing Grace's location. The unit remains active. RE10 could feature this unit (or a sister unit from the same BSAA storage) as recurring threat.

My take

The Mr. X sequence is RE9's most successful nostalgia integration. Capcom didn't try to make Mr. X scarier than RE2 Remake's version. They recreated the dynamic faithfully and earned the chapter 6 callback. If you played RE2 Remake, this is the chapter 6 highlight.
